Who should I collaborate with?

  • A teacher?
  • Multiple teachers?
  • Students?

A single teacher

This would be a good place to start. This will allow you to get your feet wet, and test out the waters to see if this is something for you.

Multiple teachers

This might be a good idea to branch into in the future. This could look differently depending on how you approach this idea. You could have multiple teachers giving input into the creation of a single project. Or, you can have multiple individiual projects going at the same time. I've found the latter to be easier, but will again attempt the former in the future.

Students

The basis of this is to create a project (or multiple projects) based on the requests of students in someone else's classroom. I have found this to be the most rewarding for both sets of students.

What class(es) do I run this project in?

I've had success in both ICS3U and ICS4U

For ICS3U, the students created a product for a primary school teacher for their mathematics class

For ICS4U, each student (or group) created a product for a primary school student group

How do I go about doing this myself?

put in information about what I did then take a moment and ask what other people have done. This will be a good time to switch over to the Google Doc in order to start taking notes from anyone in the audience. Give the Google Doc to the audience to work with. For the Google Doc, ensure that you section it off to include various questions for the audience to partake in. This will ensure that they have some questions to think about and a path for them to think about implementation in their own classroom. This will chew up the remainder of the time in the lecture.
